## Summary

- Cover crops can reduce nitrate leaching in EU-relevant cropping systems, but the size of the effect depends on species, site, and weather.
- Legume cover crops can increase soil N availability and sometimes yield of the following crop, but they may also increase nitrate leaching if residual N is mineralized during wetter periods.
- Cover crops do not consistently reduce nitrous oxide emissions; some cover crops or management choices can increase emissions, especially around winter or freeze-thaw periods.

## Strengths

- The literature consistently supports cover crops as a tool for reducing nitrate leaching and retaining soil N, especially when fields would otherwise be bare. [C001: observation] [C002: observation]
- Legume-based systems can add plant-available N, but that benefit can come with leaching or N2O penalties if residues mineralize under unfavorable conditions. [C002: observation] [C003: observation]

## Weaknesses

- N2O responses are inconsistent across species, seasons, and termination methods, so climate benefits are not guaranteed. [C003: observation] [C004: inference]
- Much of the evidence comes from non-EU sites, so direct transfer to EU conditions should be cautious. [C001: observation] [C002: observation] [C003: observation]

## Implications

- For EU nutrient management, cover crops are best treated as a conditional N-loss mitigation practice rather than a universal fix. [C001: observation] [C003: observation] [C005: recommendation]
- Design choices such as species mix, residue management, and termination timing matter as much as cover-crop presence itself. [C002: observation] [C003: observation] [C004: inference]

## Recommendations

- **Use cover crops to intercept residual soil nitrate in EU rotations where winter leaching risk is high, especially when bare fallow would otherwise persist.** [C005: recommendation] [C001: observation]
- **Prefer site-specific species selection and residue-termination planning, with extra caution for legume residues and frost-killed covers in N-fertilised systems.** [C005: recommendation] [C002: observation] [C003: observation]
- **If the goal is both N retention and broader ecosystem services, test cover crop mixtures on-farm before scaling up.** [C005: recommendation] [C004: inference]
